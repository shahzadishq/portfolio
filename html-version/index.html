04 Process

A process with no surprises.

Fixed quotes, weekly updates, and a definition of "done" we agree on before I write a line of code. Here's how a typical engagement runs.

Start with step 01
  1. 01

    Discover & audit

    Week 0

    A free 30-minute call plus a technical audit of your current site — speed, SEO health, quick wins. You get the findings whether we work together or not.

  2. 02

    Blueprint

    Week 1

    Sitemap, keyword map, wireframes and a fixed quote. Every page gets a job description: what it ranks for, who it speaks to, what it converts.

  3. 03

    Build

    Weeks 2–5

    WordPress or Elementor build on a staging site with weekly review links. You watch it come together; nothing lands as a surprise.

  4. 04

    Optimize

    Week 6

    Speed hardening, schema, redirects, analytics, Search Console. The site launches already passing Core Web Vitals — not "we'll fix it later."

  5. 05

    Launch & grow

    Ongoing

    Monitored launch, training for your team, and an optional care plan with monthly reporting — so the site keeps earning long after day one.

08 FAQ

Fair questions.

Everything clients usually ask before we start. Anything else — just write.

johnwpmanage@gmail.com
How much does a project cost?

Most business websites land between $2,500 and $8,000; larger WooCommerce or migration projects are quoted individually. Every quote is fixed before we start — the number you approve is the number you pay.

How long does a typical build take?

Four to six weeks for a standard business site, including SEO setup. Speed-optimization and audit engagements usually wrap inside two weeks. You'll get a dated timeline in the blueprint — and I keep my dates.

Can you fix my existing slow site, or do I need a rebuild?

Usually, yes — most sites can hit green Core Web Vitals without a rebuild. The free audit tells you honestly which case you're in, with numbers. If a rebuild genuinely is cheaper than repair, I'll say so.

Do you work white-label with agencies?

Yes — about half my work is white-label WordPress development and SEO for agencies. NDA-friendly, your branding on all deliverables, and I never contact your clients directly.

Elementor or custom code — which will you recommend?

Whichever serves you. Elementor when your team needs to edit freely; a custom theme when performance budgets are extreme or the design demands it. Built with discipline, both pass Core Web Vitals — I ship proof either way.

What happens after launch?

Thirty days of included support, then an optional care plan: updates, backups, security, uptime monitoring and a monthly plain-English report. No lock-in — you own the site and can leave anytime.
